Tesla Earnings Per Share (EPS)

To get an idea of the success and health of Tesla's stocks, specifically in the early 2020s, we can use a known figure called the P/E Ratio. The P/E Ratio can tell us the growth likelihood of a stock depending on its value. Before that, lets first graph the Earnings Per Share (EPS) to get a better idea of Tesla's change from the 10s to the 20s.

TSLA Annual Earnings Per Share (EPS) & P/E Ratio



Hover over the bars to activate the interaction, they will activate and show the earnings per share for that year

We can see that for all of the 10s, tesla did not earn a single dollar from its shares. Sure it was doing amazing work, but there simply wasn't a large enough market to really build on the concept & move to a high production status. Because of the Model S's popularity and Tesla's key choices to build vehicles that a larger consumer base could afford, Tesla started to turn a profit. Between 2021 and 2022 they more than doubled their EPS.

If we take a look at the table above, we can see the calculated P/E Ratio based on our graphed EPS and average Price Per Share for each year. The ratios are extremely high, with the average technology company having a P/E ratio of around 30. This signifies its high potential of growth as the larger the P/E the more likelihood of growth.
